Love Letter

My love, Jasim!

For a few days now, I’ve felt it, your silence sitting between us like a closed door. Maybe you really are ignoring me, or maybe it’s exactly what you said: that I don’t exist for you anymore. And if that is your truth, it’s okay.

If you want me to stay away, I will. No matter how much it hurts. Because between the two of us, if only one heart can be at peace, I want it to be yours. Your happiness matters more to me than my ache ever will. I will love you quietly, I will cherish you from afar, and I will never come in your way, unless you ever choose my company again.

I know you probably won’t read this. And that is exactly why I’m finally letting my heart speak without fear of interruption.

You once told me my feelings are fake. Maybe they are, maybe I don’t even know how to prove what lives inside my chest. All I know is this: even after your little effort, even after the distance you placed so easily, my heart still wants to fight for “us.” It still wants to hold on, still wants to put in effort, still wants to believe we could stay together. If this is fake, then why does it feel like my ribs are holding a storm?

I have never wanted to hurt anyone. I have always tried to make sure that if people can’t be happy because of me, at least they are not sad because of me. And yet here I am, wondering if this is a punishment or simply my fate: to love someone who doesn’t love me back.

They say love is something Allah places in hearts. And maybe Allah placed love for you in mine… and forgot to place it in yours. Because one-sided love is only pain, nothing else. But I don’t have another road to walk. I only have this: to keep living while carrying you like a wound I refuse to hate.

I don’t know why I keep finding you in everyone. In strangers’ laughter, in familiar streets, in songs that have nothing to do with you, yet somehow become you. I try to move forward, but my heart keeps turning back, like it left something unfinished in your hands.

And still… I can’t deny what we had.

The time I spent with you will always remain the best time of my life. I don’t regret meeting you, not even for a second, whether you stay with me or not. Some people become memories that don’t fade; they become a quiet kind of forever. You are that for me.

Your touch is precious to me, not just the way your hand felt in mine, but the way your presence once made me feel safe in my own skin. I will try my level best to preserve that tenderness inside me, to keep it pure, to keep it unbroken, even if you never return.

And if love had a language beyond words, I would write you in that. I would write you in the soft hush of dawn, in the warmth that lingers after a prayer, in the kind of silence that doesn’t feel empty, only holy. I wanted you and me to be us. Not a maybe. Not an almost. Not a lesson. I wanted a home.

I wanted to experience your love the way I have loved you, without doubt, without bargaining, without feeling like I am too much. I wanted to be held without having to beg the world for proof that I matter. I wanted your heart to choose me the way mine has chosen you, again and again, even when it hurt.

And my prayer is this: if there is a next life, may I meet you first, before you fell in love with that girl. So I can show you, from the beginning, what you always were to me: not an option, but a priority. So I can love you in a way that never makes you question your worth. So you can know, truly know, that I would never place anyone above you.

If this life is the one where I love you from a distance, then I will do it with dignity. I will not chase you into resentment. I will not turn my love into noise. I will simply carry it, softly, silently, like a candle that keeps burning even when no one is watching.

You can come to me anytime, no matter what season of life you’re in, and you will always find me still yours.

Always loving you.

Always longing for you.

Shehar Bano

Happy Birthday

Jasim
